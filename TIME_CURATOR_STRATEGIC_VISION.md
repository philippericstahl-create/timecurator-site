## 2. Website Design Rules

### Absolute Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **NEVER put text on the watch dial** — The dial is sacred. Use the dark corners and edges around the watch for text placement.
2. **Logo in bottom-left corner** — Not centered, not on the dial. Bottom-left where there's dark space.
3. **Use all corners creatively** — Bottom-left for logo, top-right and bottom-right for annotations and info.
4. **Screen-filling hero images** — One watch, full viewport. No split layouts.
5. **Darker, moodier images** — Add contrast, darken the dials. More dramatic, more cinematic.
6. **One cohesive color palette** — Gold/warm tones from the watches. No mixing of random reds, yellows, greens.
7. **Vintage red, not bright red** — Match the actual bezel color of the GMT-Master. Authentic, not digital.

### Design Principles

- **3D, not 2D** — Depth, shadows, layers. Not a flat grid like Chrono24.
- **Movement and animation** — Text sliding in, highlights appearing, Ken Burns zoom on images.
- **Full-width usage** — No dead black space on the right side. Use every pixel.
- **Personal voice** — "I" not "we" or "our." This is Philipp Stahl's business.
- **Show, don't tell** — "What We Do" should be a video clip, not text. People don't read.

### Hero Section — Storytelling Annotations

Lines extending from the watch movement outward, pointing to details:
- "Ref. 6542 · c. 1955"
- "Original Bakelite Bezel"
- "~650 Known Examples"
- "Untouched · Unpolished"

Each hero slide should have its own unique annotations matching the watch shown.

### "How We Sell" — The Time Curator Sales Process

This replaces "Pioneer Projects" and is the core value proposition:

**"This Is How We Calculate the Price of Your Watch"**

The selling timeline:
- **Week 1-2:** Private placement on the Time Curator podium (inner circle, serious collectors)
- **Week 3-4:** Online through the network (broader reach, RPM* marketplace)
- **Announcement → Promotion → Sale → Result sharing**

Key messaging:
- Some grails need time — not everyone checks their phone daily
- Some pieces need newspaper features, proper promotion
- 2 weeks private + 2 weeks online = 4 weeks total cycle
- Announce it, promote it, share the result publicly

This positions Time Curator in the **10-15% premium market** alongside auction house private sales, but with personal guidance.

## 4. The Collector's Office — Membership Platform

### Core Concept

The Collector's Office is not just a tagline — it's the product. An "office" where collectors:
- Make deals (5-10% commission market)
- Access expertise 24/7
- Build their collections with guidance

### Membership Tiers (Conceptual)

| Tier | What You Get | Positioning |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Bronze** | Website access, market reports, newsletter | Entry level |
| **Silver** | + AI advisor 24/7, priority grail notifications | Serious collector |
| **Gold** | + AR glasses, real-time authentication, personal curation | Inner circle |
| **Platinum** | + Direct line to Philipp, first access, event invitations | The elite |

### Age-Based Collector Templates

Templates designed for different collector profiles, identified by age:
- **Young + wealthy (20s):** Fast buyers, passion-driven, high volume. Want everything NOW.
- **Mid-career (40s-50s):** Strategic, building legacy collections. Patient but decisive.
- **Established (60+):** Curating, refining, possibly selling. Value expertise and trust.

Each template offers different UI, recommendations, and communication style.

## 5. The AR Glasses — The Golden Trick

### Vision

Smart glasses (Ray-Ban Meta style, but with inside display) as the membership device:

- **Always-on companion** — No stress about "going online." It's ambient, relaxed.
- **Real-time authentication** — Point at a watch, get instant analysis
- **Pricing overlays** — Market data, comparable sales, RPR* valuation
- **Provenance checks** — History, ownership chain, red flags
- **24/7 AI advisor** — Trained on Philipp's 37 years of expertise. Answers 99.9% of questions.
- **Personalized curation** — "Based on your collection, you might like..."

### Business Model

- Glasses could be given for free to justify premium membership pricing

- All data flows through the Time Curator network
- Every interaction = better recommendations = more trust = more deals
